the blackOUT blockparty

Problem Space

the blackOUT company is a media theatre company in Atlanta that provides a safe space for black and queer upcoming artist like their most recent event, the 1st annual blackOUT block party. The block party was a covid-safe drive-in performance and charity event that could be viewed either virtually or in-person. While the safety of the artist was secured by having a pre-recorded show, they still required safety measures for ordering food and merchandise from vendors. We partnered with the blackOUT company to develop low-cost and free solutions to scale their ticket and merchandise purchases, host the virtual performance, ensure the safety of all parties involved.

Strategy

Illum recognizes that many upcoming businesses have limited resources and cannot afford traditional methods for online stores and ticketing. We chose to prioritize this measure while brainstorming solutions. We developed several free and existing alternatives that would help aid in sales and marketing.

Time Constraint

2 Weeks

Tools

Solutions

Increasing ticket sales through Eventbrite

The blackOUT company typically relies on ticket sales via walk-up and social media marketing. By introducing them to eventbrite, they were able to sell out all in-person tickets.

Improving Vendor Engagement via Splashthat

We then used Splashthat to host information about the event for vendors. This resulted in a 10% increase with vendor engagement.

JotForm for Online Ordering

Traditional online stores require a small transaction percentage. As an alternative, we decided to host all vendor menus on the jotform and create order forms to purchase merchandise and food. Once a customer submitted the request, it would be sent to “runners” who delivered their orders to their car and received payment significantly reducing the number of interactions amongst people. Roughly 15% of their profits were acquired from vendors. Also, 20% of vendors sold out of their products without ever having to be in contact with a customer.
