Our Pillars

Intersectionality

Wait! What is intersectionality?

Intersectionality is a theoretical framework that carefully examines the complex nature of related and interdependent social groupings such as race, gender, class, sexual orientation, and religion. Through understanding the nature of people’s overlapping identities and experiences we are able to understand the complexity of prejudices they face.


Soo…. What does that have to do with technology?

Technology is often created in the reflection of its users. However, many research studies show that users within marginalized groups are often overlooked or ignored during the design process. By taking an intersectional design approach, we help companies reach marginalized users that are traditionally neglected by conventional design practices prompting designers to carefully examine and eliminate their own biases.

Empathy

Empathy— one of the most important traits humans have the ability to demonstrate, is what guides us through each step of our design process. One of our biggest advantages is our ability to understand and share another person's experiences and emotions. Our personal experiences and backgrounds position us to be able to empathize with marginalized communities often forgotten and left behind by technology pushing us to work towards creating technology that successfully uplifts their needs and wants.
